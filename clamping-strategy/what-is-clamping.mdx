---
title: "Understand clamping strategy"
description: "Clamping Strategy is where you define how the part will be held on the machine for each setup."
---
Every machinist knows this from years in the shop: when you machine a part, you have to hold it. The fixture — whether it is a vise, clamps, chuck, or dedicated fixture plate — physically covers some faces of the part. Those covered faces **cannot be machined** in that setup.

In Neuramill, you define a **clamping strategy** for each setup. A strategy tells Neuramill:

1. Which face is sitting down on the fixture (**the datum face**)
2. What kind of fixture you are using
3. Which other faces the fixture is touching (**blocked faces**)

Neuramill uses this to check that every detected feature is accessible in at least one of your strategies. Any feature that is not covered by any strategy shows up as **Remaining** — it would not get machined if you left it that way.

**Your goal: get the Remaining count to zero.**

## A simple two-setup example

Most prismatic parts (rectangular blocks) need at least two setups:

| Setup | Part Position | What Gets Machined |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Setup 1** | Part resting on Bottom face | Everything accessible from the Top, Front, Back, Left, Right |
| **Setup 2** | Flip the part — now resting on Top face | Everything accessible from the Bottom |

You define both setups as separate strategies in Neuramill. The system checks that between the two strategies, every feature is covered.
