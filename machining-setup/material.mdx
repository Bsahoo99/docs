The **Material** dropdown is in the top-left section of the Machining Setup panel.

## Select Your Material

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click the Material dropdown">
    Click the dropdown field labeled **MATERIAL**. A list of materials appears.
    
    The list is grouped by material type: Aluminium alloys, Steel alloys, Titanium, and others. Scroll to find your material.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find your exact alloy">
    Each material name includes its alloy code and hardness range — for example:
    - *FX-T2 — 353–400 HB*
    - *Aluminium 6061-T6 — 95–100 HB*
    
    Choose the most specific match you can find.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click your material to select it">
    Click the material name. The dropdown closes and shows your selected material.
    A **Material Properties** panel expands below, showing the key properties of that material.
    
    ![Material dropdown open with expanded material properties](/images/onboarding/screenshots/s31-machining-material-dropdown-properties.webp)
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Material Properties Explained

| Property | Example Value | What It Means |
|----------|--------------|---------------|
| **Hardness** | 353 – 400 HB | How hard the material is. Higher = harder = lower cutting speeds needed. |
| **Yield Strength** | 950 – 1000 MPa | The stress at which the material starts to permanently deform. |
| **Tensile Strength** | 1100 – 1150 MPa | The maximum stress before the material breaks. |
| **Elongation** | 15 – 16% | How much it stretches before breaking — higher means more ductile (bendy). |
| **Modulus of Elasticity** | 203 – 205 GPa | How stiff the material is — how much it resists bending under load. |

<Tip>
**Be as specific as possible.** Select the exact alloy code if it is in the list. "Aluminium" is much less useful than "Aluminium 6061-T6." The more specific you are, the more accurate the speed and feed recommendations will be.
</Tip>
