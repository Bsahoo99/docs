The **Machining Parameters** section on each tool card gives you three numbers. These are the numbers you program into your CNC machine.

![Machining parameters on a tool card: spindle speed, cutting feedrate, and plunge feedrate](/images/onboarding/screenshots/s54-itr-machining-parameters.webp)

## Three parameters

| Parameter | What It Is | How You Use It at the Machine |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------------------|
| **Spindle Speed (RPM)** | How fast the tool spins — revolutions per minute. Example: 8,000 RPM | Enter as your **S word** in the G-code program: `S8000` |
| **Cutting Feedrate** (mm/min or IPM) | How fast the tool moves sideways through the material. Example: 1,200 mm/min | Enter as your **F word** on the cutting moves: `F1200` |
| **Plunge Feedrate** (mm/min or IPM) | How fast the tool moves straight down into the material. Always slower than the cutting feedrate — plunging is harder on the tool. Example: 400 mm/min | Use this F word on your plunge (Z-down) moves: `F400` |

## A real-world example

Say the tool card shows:
- Spindle Speed: **8,100 RPM**
- Cutting Feedrate: **1,350 mm/min**
- Plunge Feedrate: **400 mm/min**

In your G-code program, you would write:
```
S8100 M03          (spindle on, 8100 RPM)
G01 Z-5 F400       (plunge down 5mm at 400 mm/min)
G01 X50 F1350      (cut sideways at 1350 mm/min)
```

## Your first run — start at 50%

<Warning>
**On the first time you run a new program generated from Neuramill, set your machine's feedrate override dial to 50%.**

Run the first pass. Listen to the cut. Watch the chips.

- Chips should be curling away cleanly — not powdery, not stringy
- The sound should be a consistent hum — not a screech or chatter
- The spindle load meter should be steady, not spiking

If everything looks and sounds good, ramp up to 75%, then 100%.

Neuramill gives you the calculated target values. You verify they are right for your specific setup.
</Warning>

## If the feedrate feels wrong

If the calculated feedrate seems too aggressive or too conservative for the way you work, you can override it. See [Manual Override](/tool-recommender/manual-override).

The relationship between the parameters is:
> **Feedrate = RPM × Flutes × Chip Load**

If you change RPM, check the feedrate too — they are linked.
