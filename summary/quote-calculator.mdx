Quick Quote turns a finished plan into a rough price. It combines the cycle time Neuramill calculated with rates you enter — shop rate, material, tooling, and fixturing — and adds your margin to produce a quote.

<Info>
Quick Quote is an early preview and may change. Treat its output as a rough estimate, not a committed price.
</Info>

You open it from the **Summary** step, so finish the plan first. See [Review the session summary](/summary/summary-view) and [Estimate cycle time](/summary/cycle-time).

## Open Quick Quote

In the **Summary** step, find the cycle time area and click the **Quote** button (calculator icon). A draggable **Quick Quote** panel opens. Drag the panel by its top bar to move it, and close it with the **✕** when you are done.

## Session context

The top of the panel shows values pulled from your plan. These are read-only.

| Value | Where it comes from |
|-------|---------------------|
| Machining time | The total machining time from the cycle time estimate |
| Setup time | The total setup time from the cycle time estimate |
| Setups | The number of clamping strategies in the plan |
| Tools used | The number of unique tools selected |
| Stock volume | Calculated from your stock dimensions, shown in cubic inches |

## Your rates

Enter your shop's rates. Every field is optional — a cost line only appears in the quote when you give it a value.

| Field | Unit | Used for |
|-------|------|----------|
| Shop Rate | $/hr | Machine time and setup cost |
| Material Cost | $/lb | Material cost |
| Material Density | lb/in³ | Material cost |
| Avg Tool Price | $ | Tool wear cost |
| Avg Tool Life | min | Tool wear cost |
| Fixture Cost | $ | A flat fixturing charge |

<Warning>
Quick Quote uses imperial units only — dollars per pound, pounds per cubic inch, and dollars per hour. The viewer unit switch (mm/in) does not change these fields.
</Warning>

## Outside processing

Add any work done outside your shop — for example anodizing, heat treat, or plating. Click **Add Process**, give it a name and a cost, and it becomes a line in the quote. Remove a line with the **✕** next to it.

## Margin

Enter a **Margin** percentage. It is applied to the subtotal of all cost lines to produce the final quote.

## How the quote is calculated

Each cost is a line item; lines with a value above zero are summed into a subtotal, and your margin is added on top.

| Line item | How it is estimated |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Material | Stock volume × material density × material cost per pound |
| Machine Time | Machining hours × shop rate |
| Setup | Setup hours × shop rate |
| Tool Wear | (Total cutting minutes ÷ average tool life) × average tool price |
| Fixture | The flat fixture cost you entered |
| Outside processes | Each process cost you added |

The panel then shows:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Subtotal">
    The sum of every cost line above.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Margin">
    The subtotal multiplied by your margin percentage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Quote">
    Subtotal plus margin — your estimated price.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
The estimate is only as good as the rates you enter and the cycle time estimate it builds on. Review your [cycle time](/summary/cycle-time) and setup times before you rely on a quote.
</Note>
