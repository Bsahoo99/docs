title: "Use view controls"
description: "Tools #4 through #8 — reset view, grid, wireframe, snap to face, and stock overlay."
---
These five tools control how the 3D model looks and what overlays are visible.

## Tool #4 — Reset View

**What it does:** Returns the camera to the default starting angle and zoom level.

**When to use it:** Any time you have rotated or zoomed far in and you want to get back to the standard view quickly. This is also the fastest way to clear a measurement made with the Measure tool.

## Tool #5 — Toggle Grid

**What it does:** Turns the background grid floor on and off.

**When to use it:** The grid helps you judge the scale and orientation of your part. If it is in the way or making the view cluttered, click this to hide it.

## Tool #6 — Toggle Wireframe

**What it does:** Switches the 3D model between solid view (you see surfaces) and wireframe view (you see only the edges and outlines).

**When to use it:** Wireframe mode lets you see through the part to features on the far side — useful for checking holes, pockets, and other internal geometry.

## Tool #7 — Snap to Face

**What it does:** Snaps the camera to look straight at the currently selected or highlighted face. The camera centers on that face and looks directly at it from outside.

**When to use it:** When you want to confirm exactly which features are on a specific face. Click the Top face, then press Snap to Face — the camera snaps to a straight top-down view. Click Front, press Snap to Face — you get a straight front view.

<Tip>
Think of this like centering your view on a face. It is the same as pressing "Top View" or "Front View" on your CAD software.
</Tip>

## Tool #8 — Toggle Stock

**What it does:** Turns the transparent stock box overlay on and off. The stock overlay is a semi-transparent grey box showing the raw material dimensions you entered in the Machining Setup step.

**When to use it:** Use it to visually confirm that the stock box fully encloses your part. If part of the model sticks outside the box, your stock dimensions are too small and need to be increased.
