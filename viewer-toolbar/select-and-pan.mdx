---
title: "Use select and pan"
description: "Tool #1 selects features. Tool #2 pans the camera. Here is how to use both."
---
## Tool #1 — Select

The **Select** tool lets you click on a face or feature on the 3D model to select and highlight it.

**When to use it:** Any time you want to click on a specific face of the part — for example, to see which features are on that face, or to move a feature to a different direction in the Direction of Cut step.

**How to use it:**
1. Click tool **#1** (the arrow cursor icon) to make sure Select is the active tool.
2. Move your mouse over the 3D model.
3. Click on the face or feature you want to select. It highlights in blue.

## Tool #2 — Pan

The **Pan** tool moves the camera position left, right, up, and down — without rotating the part.

**When to use it:** When you have zoomed in close and you want to slide the view to see a different area of the part without changing the angle.

**How to use it:**
1. Click tool **#2** (the four-arrow icon) to make Pan the active tool.
2. Click and hold anywhere on the 3D viewer.
3. While holding, drag your mouse in the direction you want to move. The view follows.
4. Release the mouse when you reach the position you want.

## Rotating the Part (No Tool Required)

You do not need to select any tool to rotate the part. Just:
1. Click and hold on the **grey background** area of the viewer (not on the part itself).
2. Drag in any direction. The part rotates.
3. Release when you have the angle you want.

**Zoom in and out:** Scroll your mouse wheel forward to zoom in, backward to zoom out.
