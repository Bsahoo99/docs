The toolbar sits at the very top of the 3D viewer area. It stays visible through all five steps of your session — the same tools are always there.

The toolbar has **8 numbered tools**, a **gear icon** on the far left (viewer display settings), and a **mm / in switcher** on the far right.

![Full 3D viewer toolbar with controls labeled](/images/onboarding/screenshots/s20-viewer-toolbar-overview-labeled.webp)

## All 8 tools at a glance

| Tool # | Name | What It Does |
|--------|------|--------------|
| 1 | **Select** | Click a face or feature on the 3D model to select and highlight it |
| 2 | **Pan** | Move the camera left/right/up/down without rotating the part |
| 3 | **Measure** | Click two points on the model to measure the distance between them |
| 4 | **Reset View** | Return the camera to the default angle and zoom |
| 5 | **Toggle Grid** | Turn the background grid floor on and off |
| 6 | **Toggle Wireframe** | Switch between solid view and wireframe (edges only) view |
| 7 | **Snap to Face** | Snap the camera to look straight at the currently selected face |
| 8 | **Toggle Stock** | Turn the transparent stock box overlay on and off |

## Active tool

The currently active tool highlights (turns blue or has a border around it). Only one tool can be active at a time. Click a different tool to switch.
