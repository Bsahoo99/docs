---
title: "Classify complex surfaces"
description: "How to select multiple faces at once and classify them together as an OD Profile or 3D Surface."
---
For parts with complex outer profiles or large curved surfaces that span multiple faces, you can **select multiple faces at once** and classify them together as a single feature.

## When to use this

- Your part has a **stepped or profiled outer contour** that wraps around multiple faces → use **OD Profile**
- Your part has **sculptured or free-form surfaces** that require 3D toolpaths → use **3D Surface**

## Select multiple faces

<Steps>
  <Step title="Hold Ctrl (or Cmd on Mac)">
    Hold the **Ctrl** key on your keyboard (or **Cmd** if you are on a Mac).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click each face you want to include">
    While holding Ctrl/Cmd, click on each face of the 3D model that should be part of this feature.
    Each face you click highlights in a different color to show it is selected.
    
    ![Multiple faces selected with OD Profile and 3D Surface options](/images/onboarding/screenshots/s29-direction-multi-select-advanced-classification.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="See the floating bar at the bottom">
    Once you have selected faces, a **floating bar** appears at the very bottom of the 3D viewer area. It shows two buttons: **OD Profile** and **3D Surface**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click OD Profile or 3D Surface">
    Click the classification that matches your feature.
    A submenu appears asking you to choose the **setup direction** (Top, Bottom, Front, Back, Left, Right).
    As you hover over each direction option, the selected faces preview in a different color — this helps you confirm the right approach direction.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the direction">
    Click your chosen direction.
    A new feature card is created for that classification and added to the feature list.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
If you selected the wrong faces, click anywhere on the grey background (away from the model) to deselect everything, then start the selection again.
</Tip>
