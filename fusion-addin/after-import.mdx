After the import finishes, your document holds the setups, tools, and operations from your plan. From here you work in Fusion as usual — generate toolpaths, simulate, post-process, and export NC code.

## Toolpaths

If you left **Generate toolpaths after import** checked, Fusion generates the toolpaths once the operations are built — no extra step needed.

If you unchecked it, or you want to regenerate after a change, select the setups or operations in the CAM browser and use Fusion's **Generate Toolpath** command.

## See what each operation machines

When you select an operation or setup in Fusion's CAM browser, the add-in highlights the faces that operation machines. Use this to confirm each operation targets the geometry you expect before you cut.

## Simulate, post-process, and export NC

Continue with Fusion's standard CAM workflow:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Simulate">
    Run Fusion's **Simulate** to check toolpaths, stock removal, and collisions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Post-process">
    Use **Post Process** with your machine's post to generate NC code.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export NC code">
    Save the NC file and load it on your machine.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Need to bring in another plan? [Generate a fresh export key](/fusion-addin/generating-an-export-key) — keys are one-time use.
</Note>
