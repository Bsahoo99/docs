The **Policies** area lists the machining policies that drive Neuramill's recommendations. A policy maps a combination of feature, material, and machine to a set of operation parameters — for example roughing depth, finishing depth-of-cut, or thread-milling rules. When you plan a part, Neuramill uses these policies to decide which operations and parameters to suggest.

Any team member can view and manage policies.

![Policies list with type, tier, and clamping strategy columns](/images/onboarding/screenshots/s68-dashboard-policies-list.webp)

## Policy details

Each policy in the list shows the following columns.

| Column | What it means |
|--------|---------------|
| Policy type | The kind of machining rule, such as roughing, finishing, or thread milling. |
| Tier | How aggressively tools are chosen for this policy — **Ideal**, **Moderate**, or **Worst**. Neuramill ships defaults, and you can customize them to match your tool-selection preference. |
| Clamping strategies | The clamping strategies this policy is associated with. |

## When to use this area

Policies are advanced settings. Most planning work does not require you to open this area — Neuramill applies the relevant policy automatically as you move through a session.

<Note>
Policies map features and materials to operation parameters. To review the libraries that feed those mappings, see [Machining configuration](/dashboard/machining-config).
</Note>
